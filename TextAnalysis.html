Now let’s talk about the similarities in character development and how the formatting of each text plays a part in how the reader may view the characters. The encoding process of my text some of which can be see above in the quote example really helped me see learn the formatting of the text and really analyze the character development in The Great Gatsby and be able to compare and contrast it to the other text. For example, in the markup I decided to mark up the places in The Great Gatsby the places being East Egg and West Egg I learned that East Egg village symbolizes old money, and West Egg symbolizes new money. this encoding allows me to understand the differences in the character for example in the text I encoded it states I lived at West Egg, the—well, the less fashionable of the two, though this is a most superficial tag to express the bizarre and not a little sinister contrast between them. My house was at the very tip of the egg, only fifty yards from the Sound, and squeezed between two huge places that rented for twelve or fifteen thousand a season. By this encoding of places we can see how living in the West Egg for Gatsby was a grand accomplishment and expensive because the places rented for thousands of dollars. And as I walked on, I was lonely no longer.” In this quote he expresses how some days are lonely in a big space, and he wishes he has someone to share it with, and the excitement of the possibility.
